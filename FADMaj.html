D Major Chord

The D major chord is a bright and uplifting chord that is often used in folk, rock, and pop music.
It is a great chord for beginners to learn as it is relatively easy to play and sounds great on the guitar. To the right, you can see a diagram for this chord.
Basics:

To play the D major chord, you will need to place your fingers on the following frets:

  • 2st fret of the G string
  • 3nd fret of the B string
  • 2rd fret of the high e string

When strumming the D major chord, make sure to only strum the top four strings
(D, G, B, and high E).
This will give you a clean and clear sound.
